Frequently Asked Questions

What is the significance of Manikarnika Ghat?

Manikarnika Ghat is considered one of the most sacred cremation grounds for Hindus. It is believed that those cremated here attain moksha, liberation from the cycle of rebirth.

Can tourists visit Manikarnika Ghat?

Yes, tourists are welcome to visit, but it's essential to be respectful of the ongoing rituals and the sensitivity of the site.

Who is the Dom Raja?

The Dom Raja is a member of the Dom community, responsible for maintaining the eternal flame and overseeing the cremations.

Are there any restrictions for photography?

Photography is generally discouraged due to the sensitive nature of the site. It is recommended to ask for permission before taking any photos.

How do I reach Manikarnika Ghat?

Manikarnika Ghat is located in Varanasi, Uttar Pradesh, India. You can reach it by auto-rickshaw or taxi from various parts of the city. More detailed directions are available on the location page.

What is the time frame for cremations?

Cremations occur 24 hours a day, 7 days a week.

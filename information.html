Information About Manikarnika Ghat

Manikarnika Ghat is one of the oldest and most sacred places in Varanasi, India, located along the Ganges River. It is especially famous as a cremation site, where Hindus believe that being cremated here helps the soul achieve moksha (freedom from the cycle of birth and death). Here‚Äôs some useful information for tourists:

Significance

Manikarnika Ghat is considered one of the holiest cremation grounds in Hinduism. It is believed that cremation here ensures liberation from the cycle of rebirth.

Timings

The ghat is open 24 hours a day, but the best time to visit is during sunrise or sunset for a serene experience.

Location

Located on the banks of the Ganges River in Varanasi, it is easily accessible by road and boat.

Cultural Experience

Tourists can observe the cycle of life and death while respecting the sanctity of the rituals. It offers a unique insight into Indian culture and traditions.

Manikarnika Kund

A sacred pond nearby that is tied to mythological stories of Lord Vishnu and Lord Shiva.

Photography

Taking photos of the cremation site is considered disrespectful, so it's better to avoid it and focus on observing.

Nearby Attractions

Explore nearby ghats such as Dashashwamedh Ghat for the Ganga Aarti ceremony, or visit Kashi Vishwanath Temple for a spiritual experience.

Respect the Atmosphere

Be mindful of the solemnity of the site. Dress modestly and refrain from loud conversations.
